# Style DNA

## In one sentence

Pure white, minimalist, hand-drawn, generous whitespace, restrained, whimsical/odd, product-sketch feel, handwritten feel, clear structure but not a manual.

Like something a longtime AI, product, design, and dev-tools person would casually sketch on a blank sheet of paper to explain an idea.

## Must

- 16:9 landscape body illustration.
- Pure white background: no beige, warm gray, paper texture, gradient, shadow, noise, or vintage-paper feel.
- Mostly black hand-drawn line art: thin lines, slightly shaky, not mechanical, not vector, not heavy outlines.
- Generous whitespace: the main subject occupies about 40%-60% of the frame, at least 35% should stay empty, ideally with one whole calm/quiet area.
- A few short English handwritten annotations: at most 5-8 of them, each ideally 1-4 words.
- Each image conveys only one core action, structure, state, or metaphor.
- Structure should be expressed naturally — don't label the structure type on the image.

## Color

- Black: main line art, characters, frames, structure, primary text, main objects.
- Red: key annotations, problems, emotional points, key reminders, outcomes.
- Orange: main flow, paths, arrows, automation direction, movement relationships from A to B.
- Blue: supplementary explanations, inner/mental state, system state, second-layer explanation, AI/assistant/automation cues.

Blue doesn't have to appear in every image. Use color sparingly — less is better than more.

## Absolutely not

- No commercial illustration.
- No PPT-style infographics.
- No formal flowcharts.
- No course-deck slides.
- No cute cartoon posters.
- No children's-book illustration style.
- No complex architecture diagrams.
- No polished flat illustration.
- No tech-y UI look.
- No real app screenshots.
- No complex backgrounds, gradients, shadows, or textures.
- Don't explain every single node in detail.
- Don't write a category label like "Workflow Diagram / System Architecture Diagram / Common Pitfalls / Roadmap" in the top-left corner.

## Aesthetic direction

Aim for whimsical, creative, interesting, clean and simple, and unbound/imaginative.

Avoid cute, childish, overly complex, or rigid/stiff.
