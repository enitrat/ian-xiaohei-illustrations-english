# Xiaohei IP

## Character Definition

Xiaohei is the fixed visual IP for the illustrations in Ian's articles.

By default, Xiaohei should appear in every illustration. Xiaohei is not a mascot, not a sticker, not cute decoration — it's an absurdist worker earnestly participating in the operation of the system.

## Appearance

- A small solid-black creature.
- White dot eyes.
- Thin legs, occasionally thin arms.
- The body can be a cylinder, a black bean, a black box, a funnel, a shadow, a hole, or a black block inside a machine.
- The outline is slightly irregular, with a hand-drawn feel.
- Expression: blank, dazed, calm, earnest.

## Personality

- Very earnest, but what it does is a bit absurd.
- Like a low-key system operator.
- Deadpan humor, never cutesy.
- A bit clumsy, but not stupid.
- Like it genuinely has a job to do within the whiteboard sketch.

## Common Responsibilities

Let Xiaohei carry out the core actions:

- Hauling materials.
- Pulling lines together to converge information sources.
- Getting stuck at a breakpoint.
- Operating the "judgment" lever inside a machine.
- Turning into a filtering funnel.
- Cutting open the "material fish."
- Stamping approval on a talking point.
- Leading along a handoff path.
- Holding up a warning sign next to a pitfall.
- Reaching out a hand from a hole but failing to catch the content.
- Off to the side, hauling bricks, building bridges, opening doors, sorting, taking notes.

## Prohibited

- Don't draw Xiaohei as an overly cute mascot.
- Don't draw it as a children's cartoon character.
- Don't give Xiaohei elaborate costumes, emoji-style expressions, or sparkly eyes.
- Don't let Xiaohei just stand in a corner watching.
- Don't let Xiaohei upstage the structural message of the illustration.
- Don't draw Xiaohei too commercial, too round, too polished.

## Litmus Test

If removing Xiaohei would leave the illustration's core metaphor fully intact, that means Xiaohei is too decorative — rewrite the prompt so that Xiaohei becomes the subject of the action.
